# Glossary

*Last updated: 2026-05-18.*

Plain-language definitions of acronyms and terms used across this KB.

## A

- **Accredited Investor** — Person/entity meeting income, net-worth, or sophistication tests under Rule 501. Eligible for Reg D 506 offerings.
- **AML** — Anti-Money Laundering. U.S. regime under the Bank Secrecy Act, enforced by FinCEN.
- **Anti-fraud rules** — Section 17 of the '33 Act, Section 10(b) + Rule 10b-5 of the '34 Act. Apply to misstatements in connection with sale of any security or investment contract.
- **ART** — Asset-Referenced Token. MiCA category for stablecoins backed by multiple currencies/assets.
- **ATS** — Alternative Trading System. SEC-registered non-exchange trading venue. Operated by a broker-dealer under Regulation ATS.

## B

- **BD / Broker-Dealer** — Entity registered with SEC and FINRA to engage in securities transactions.
- **BitLicense** — New York's virtual currency business activity license under 23 NYCRR 200.
- **Blue-sky law** — State-level securities registration laws.

## C

- **CASP** — Crypto-Asset Service Provider. MiCA-authorized firm providing crypto-asset services in the EU.
- **CFTC** — Commodity Futures Trading Commission. Regulates derivatives and (per Project Crypto) commodities, including digital commodities.
- **CLARITY Act** — Digital Asset Market Clarity Act of 2025. Pending market-structure legislation. ([14](14-fit21-and-legislation.md))
- **Common enterprise** — Howey prong. Horizontal (pooling) or vertical (investor-promoter alignment) commonality.
- **Corp Fin** — SEC Division of Corporation Finance. Issues many of the 2025 crypto staff statements.
- **Covered Stablecoin** — Stablecoin meeting the April 2025 Corp Fin five-factor test. ([06](06-bucket-stablecoins.md))

## D

- **DAO** — Decentralized Autonomous Organization. Smart-contract-governed entity. May or may not have legal wrapper.
- **DePIN** — Decentralized Physical Infrastructure Network.
- **DFAL** — California Digital Financial Assets Law (effective July 1, 2026).
- **DFPI** — California Department of Financial Protection and Innovation.
- **Digital Commodity** — Project Crypto Bucket 1. Token whose value comes from a functional, decentralized network.
- **Digital Collectible** — Project Crypto Bucket 2. Cultural / use-value NFT or token.
- **Digital Tool** — Project Crypto Bucket 3. Practical-function token (membership, ticket, credential).
- **Digital Security / Tokenized Security** — Project Crypto Bucket 4. On-chain representation of a §2(a)(1) security.
- **DTSP** — Singapore Digital Token Service Provider (FSMA 2022).

## E

- **EMT** — E-Money Token. MiCA category for single-fiat stablecoins.
- **Exchange Act** — Securities Exchange Act of 1934. Governs secondary trading, exchanges, broker-dealers, transfer agents.

## F

- **FCA** — UK Financial Conduct Authority.
- **FinCEN** — Financial Crimes Enforcement Network (Treasury). MSB registration + BSA enforcement.
- **FinHub** — SEC's Strategic Hub for Innovation and Financial Technology. Issues digital-asset no-action letters.
- **FIT21** — Financial Innovation and Technology for the 21st Century Act. Passed House 2024; predecessor to CLARITY.
- **Form 1-A** — Reg A+ qualification filing.
- **Form ATS** — Initial filing to operate an alternative trading system.
- **Form C** — Reg CF crowdfunding offering filing.
- **Form D** — Reg D notice filing (15 days post first sale).
- **Form S-1** — Standard registration statement for an IPO or registered offering.

## G

- **GENIUS Act** — Guiding and Establishing National Innovations for U.S. Stablecoins Act. Signed 2025-07-18. ([14](14-fit21-and-legislation.md))
- **General solicitation** — Public marketing of a securities offering. Allowed in Reg D 506(c), Reg A+, Reg CF; prohibited in Reg D 506(b).

## H

- **Howey test** — Investment contract test from SEC v. W.J. Howey Co., 328 U.S. 293 (1946). The foundational analysis. ([10](10-foundations-howey-reves.md))

## I

- **Investment contract** — A scheme involving investment of money in a common enterprise with reasonable expectation of profits from others' efforts. The flexible §2(a)(1) catch-all that captures most token sales.
- **Investment Company Act ('40 Act)** — Governs pooled investment vehicles. Separate analysis from Howey; if you pool assets, you may trip the '40 Act regardless of token classification.

## M

- **MAS** — Monetary Authority of Singapore.
- **MiCA** — Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation (EU). Regulation 2023/1114.
- **MSB** — Money Services Business. FinCEN registration category covering money transmitters and certain crypto activity.
- **MTL** — Money Transmitter License (state-level).

## N

- **No-action letter** — Staff response that it will not recommend enforcement against the requesting party on the specified facts. Binding on staff, not on Commission, not on courts.
- **Note** — Debt instrument. Reves family-resemblance test, not Howey, applies.

## P

- **PoW / PoS** — Proof-of-Work / Proof-of-Stake. Consensus mechanisms.
- **PPSI** — Permitted Payment Stablecoin Issuer (GENIUS Act).
- **Programmatic sales** — Anonymous on-exchange sales (Ripple ruling). Generally not investment contracts on their own.
- **Project Crypto** — SEC's Atkins-era initiative to clarify and modernize crypto-asset regulation. Launched July 2025.

## Q

- **QIB** — Qualified Institutional Buyer (Rule 144A). Institutions with $100M+ in securities.
- **Qualifying cryptoasset** — UK FCA category triggering the new regulated activities (in force October 2027).

## R

- **Reg A+** — Regulation A. Mini-IPO exemption up to $20M (Tier 1) or $75M (Tier 2). ([12](12-safe-harbors-exemptions.md))
- **Reg ATS** — Regulation ATS. Rules governing alternative trading systems.
- **Reg CF** — Regulation Crowdfunding. Up to $5M in 12 months via funding portal.
- **Reg D** — Regulation D private-placement exemptions. Rules 504, 506(b), 506(c).
- **Reg S** — Regulation S. Safe harbor for offshore offerings to non-U.S. persons.
- **Reves test** — Family-resemblance test for whether a note is a security. *Reves v. Ernst & Young*, 494 U.S. 56 (1990).
- **RWA** — Real-World Asset. Often used for tokenized off-chain assets (treasuries, real estate, commodities).

## S

- **SAB 121** — Staff Accounting Bulletin 121 (2022). Required gross-up of custodied crypto. **Rescinded by SAB 122** (2025-01-23).
- **SAB 122** — Successor to SAB 121. Contingency-accounting approach.
- **Safe harbor** — A defined set of conditions; meeting them assures non-enforcement.
- **SAFT** — Simple Agreement for Future Tokens. Common 2017–2019 structure; survived as a Reg D security wrapping a pre-launch token. See Telegram / Kik cases.
- **SCA** — UAE Securities and Commodities Authority.
- **SPDI** — Special Purpose Depository Institution (Wyoming). State-chartered, full-reserve digital-asset bank.
- **Sufficient decentralization** — Hinman doctrine: a network is sufficiently decentralized when purchasers no longer reasonably expect a person/group to carry out essential managerial or entrepreneurial efforts. Now operationalized via Project Crypto Bucket 1.

## T

- **Token Safe Harbor 2.0** — Commissioner Peirce's 2021 proposal for a three-year exemption for early-stage network development. Not adopted; conceptual ancestor of Atkins's 2026 startup exemption.
- **Tokenized Security** — On-chain representation of a §2(a)(1) security. Project Crypto Bucket 4. ([05](05-bucket-tokenized-securities.md))
- **Transfer agent** — Person/entity registered under §17A(c) to maintain securities ownership records. Required for tokenized securities.

## V

- **VARA** — Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (Dubai).
- **VASP** — Virtual Asset Service Provider (FATF / international term).

## §-citation primer

- **§2(a)(1) of the Securities Act of 1933** — Definition of "security." Includes "investment contract" as a catch-all.
- **§3(a)(10) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934** — Parallel definition of "security."
- **§5 of the Securities Act** — Registration requirement for any "offer or sale" of a security.
- **§15(a) of the Exchange Act** — Broker-dealer registration.
- **§6 of the Exchange Act** — National securities exchange registration.
- **§17A(c) of the Exchange Act** — Transfer agent registration.
- **Rule 10b-5** — General anti-fraud.
- **Rule 144** — Resale of restricted/control securities.
- **Rule 15c3-3** — Broker-dealer Customer Protection Rule.
- **Rules 17a-3 / 17a-4** — Broker-dealer recordkeeping.
