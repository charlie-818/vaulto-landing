# SEC Token Regulation Knowledge Base

A practical reference for builders and founders tokenizing assets, organized around the SEC's "Project Crypto" five-bucket classification framework as of **May 2026**.

> ⚠️ Regulatory information only — not legal advice. The regulatory landscape changes fast. Verify every primary source before acting on it. Engage qualified securities counsel before issuing a token or operating a market.

## TL;DR — Five buckets, in plain English

As of **March 17, 2026**, the SEC (joined by the CFTC) issued Interpretive Release **33-11412 / 34-105020**, formally classifying crypto assets into five buckets ([S1], [S2], [S6]):

| Bucket | Security? | One-line test |
|--------|-----------|---------------|
| **Digital Commodities / Network Tokens** | No | Value comes from a *functional* and *decentralized* network, not from the managerial efforts of a promoter. |
| **Digital Collectibles** | No | Bought for use, enjoyment, or cultural value (art, music, in-game items, memes) — not for profit from someone else's efforts. |
| **Digital Tools** | No | Performs a practical function: membership, ticket, credential, identity badge, title instrument. |
| **Digital Securities (Tokenized Securities)** | **Yes** | Represents ownership of a financial instrument enumerated in the Securities Act §2(a)(1) or Exchange Act §3(a)(10). |
| **Stablecoins (Covered Stablecoins)** | No (with conditions) | 1:1 USD-pegged, fully backed by liquid USD reserves, redeemable on demand, no yield. |

Anti-fraud rules still apply to misstatements made in connection with selling *any* of these — even non-securities.

The April 2019 staff "Framework for Investment Contract Analysis of Digital Assets" is **superseded** by 33-11412 ([S6]).
